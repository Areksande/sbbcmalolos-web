TESTED, YET FAITHFUL

Job 2:9-10 KJV
9 Then said his wife unto him, Dost thou still retain thine integrity? curse God, and die.
10 But he said unto her, Thou speakest as one of the foolish women speaketh. What? shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil? In all this did not Job sin with his lips.
God gives us both a gift and a test. A new season does not only bring new opportunities it also reveals our integrity, our honesty, and our commitment to walk in God’s will. As we step into this new season the Lord has given us, the question is not only where God is leading us, but how we will walk with Him.
The Bible reminds us that walking in God’s will requires more than words it requires a life anchored in integrity and strengthened by commitment.

THE PRESSURE OF A NEW SEASON

Every new season tests our integrity.

The Pain of the Season

Job experienced deep loss his family, his livelihood, and his health were taken from him. New seasons often come after loss, change, or shaking. God allows pressure not to destroy us, but to reveal what is within us.

The Persuasion in the Season

Job’s wife urged him to abandon his integrity: “Curse God, and die.” In seasons of pressure, voices will rise that encourage compromise, bitterness, or withdrawal from God.

The Position of Faith in the Season

Job stood firm. He remembered God’s goodness and declared, “Shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil?” In all this, Job did not sin with his lips. Integrity is choosing faithfulness even when circumstances are unfavorable.

Respecting God’s Commandments

Christmas reminds us that God is faithful and those who follow Him must live faithfully in return. Remembering God’s goodness should lead us to respect His Word.

Devotion: Fulfilling Our Vows

Devotion is more than emotion; it is love expressed through obedience. When we truly love God, keeping His commandments becomes a joy, not a burden (John 14:15). Scripture warns us not to make promises lightly, but to fulfill what we vow to God (Ecclesiastes 5:4–5). Christ kept every promise He made His coming, His sacrifice, His resurrection, and His future return. Because He fulfilled His promises, we must honor ours.

Direction: Walking in His Ways

Once God has our heart, He desires to guide our steps. Obedience is not restriction it is protection. God’s Word lights our path, and Christ Himself is the way, the truth, and the life. Real faith is visible, lived out daily through obedience.

Distribution: Honoring God with What He Entrusted

Giving is not merely a suggestion it is a command rooted in stewardship. God does not ask for our resources because He lacks, but because we need to trust Him. Distribution flows naturally from devotion and direction. When God owns our heart, He also directs our hands.

Responding with Generous Commitment

Because Christ gave Himself fully, we are called to give joyfully.

Give God Your Heart Entirely

The heart is the control center of life. What holds our heart controls our actions. God does not want our money without our heart, nor our heart without obedience. Wholehearted devotion produces joy, while half-hearted obedience leads to frustration.

Give God Your Hands Willingly

God works through willing hands. He invites, not forces, His people to serve. From the shepherds to the wise men, Christmas faith always moves people to action. Service is the visible expression of a surrendered heart.

Give God Your Harvest Gratefully

True thanksgiving is expressed through firstfruits offering God the best, not the leftovers. The Old Testament emphasized giving the choicest portion, motivated by gratitude rather than obligation. Firstfruits represent appreciation for God’s mercy, grace, and provision.

Conclusion:

The Greatest Christmas Response Christ did His part now give Him your heart.
He left His throne
- will you give Him your time? He gave His life
- will you give Him your loyalty? He offered salvation
- will you offer surrender? He gave everything
- will you give Him your heart?

Christ has done His part. Now it is our turn give Him your heart.
